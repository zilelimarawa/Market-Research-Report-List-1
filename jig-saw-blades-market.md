<p><ul><li>T-Shank</li><li>U-Shank</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The jig saw blades market is primarily categorized into T-shank and U-shank types, reflecting their respective mounting mechanisms. T-shank blades feature a tang at the top, providing a secure fit in modern jig saws and ensuring better stability and precision during cutting. In contrast, U-shank blades have a simpler, U-shaped design, compatible with older models. The choice between these types often depends on the user's tool compatibility and the specific cutting tasks, influencing market demand and trends.</p></p>
